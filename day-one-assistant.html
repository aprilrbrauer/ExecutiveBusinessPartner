The Program

Three phases. Ninety days.
One consistent start.

Day One Ready is a structured onboarding program I designed for new EABPs at Intuit's San Diego campus — because how well a new EABP ramps shouldn't depend on luck, geography, or who they sit near.

Days 1–30

Phase 1: Orient

Know the landscape. Systems, tools, campus culture, and the exec relationship.

Days 31–60

Phase 2: Connect

Know your people. Stakeholder maps, peer introductions, cross-functional contacts.

Days 61–90

Phase 3: Operate

Work with confidence. Core workflows, craft self-assessment, development goals.

🤝 Buddy System

Every new EABP is paired with an experienced SDG EABP for the full 90 days — for scheduled touchpoints and informal questions in between.

🗓️ Five Touchpoints

Day 1 Welcome · Day 7 Debrief · Day 30 Phase Check-In · Day 60 Phase Check-In · Day 90 Graduation — all calendared on Day 1.

Behind the Build

Why I built this.

EABP onboarding at Intuit had no consistent structure. How well a new hire ramped depended entirely on circumstances outside their control — who they sat near, how organized their predecessor was, how much bandwidth their executive had. I built Day One Ready to change that.

🧠 The Knowledge Base

The assistant is trained on eight modules covering the EABP craft framework, Intuit culture, every system and tool, calendar philosophy, SDG campus logistics, finance and spend, event planning, and a full acronym glossary.

⚙️ How It's Built

Built on Claude (Anthropic) with a curated system prompt specific to the Intuit EABP experience. The knowledge base was designed collaboratively with input from experienced SDG EABPs.

📈 The Scale Vision

The SDG pilot is designed to prove the model before a broader Intuit rollout. A BU steward model keeps it sustainable — experienced EABPs own the local knowledge base and buddy pool for their team.

🎯 Success Metrics

Full productivity by Day 90. Buddy satisfaction ≥ 4/5. New EABP confidence improving from Day 1 to 90. AI assistant used at least weekly. Catherine endorses campus-wide expansion.
